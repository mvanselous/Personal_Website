About Me

Welcome to my website! I am a sophomore physics and mathematics double major at the University of Maryland, College Park.

In the fall of 2019, I was employed by the University of Maryland’s Radiation Facilities to work on our nuclear reactor and linear accelerator. With them I completed a number of projects in python, MATLAB, and AutoCAD including simulations, interactive GUI's, and the design of lab equipment. Ongoing Project

I am currently working to learn about quantum error reduction. So far, I have written a prototype quantum "majority vote" algorithm and would like to both understand its sources of error and experiment with ways to mitigate it. While, in theory, taking a majority vote should improve the success rate of a quantum circuit, in practice there are number of other factors I still need to consider. Below is a brief description of the issues I identified and will like to resolve.


Ordering of Qubits
In a quantum computer, it is not that straightforward to make two qubits communicate with each other. In the current implementation of my algorithm, I did not consider optimizing the connectivity of entangled qubits and some deceptively simple commands required several intermediate interactions. This is certainly a significant source of error I would like to address.

Error from the Gates I Used
Another major source of error comes from the individual quantum gates chosen to be implemented. I would like to develop a stronger intuition for the error provided by the individual quantum gates. That way I can design around them and anticipate their effects.

Identifying More Suitable Circuits for the Algorithm
A third factor is choosing a suitable circuit to implement an error correcting algorithm. In my current test, I have run my algorithm after a simple teleportation protocol which is relatively accurate on its own. I would like to run my algorithm in a more realistic scenario.

Learning from Experts
Of course, there are many researchers I can learn from who have most definitely created very useful error correcting algorithms. After exploring what I can on my own, I will greatly benefit from reading about their experiences.
