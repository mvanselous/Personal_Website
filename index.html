About Me

Welcome to my website! I am a junior physics and mathematics double major at the University of Maryland, College Park. I am also proficient in programming with python, MATLAB, and C++. I am familar with using GIT from the command line, Unix shells, and the windows powershell (as well as more specialized distributions such as Anaconda for the Jupyter Notebook). Experience

I have been very fortunate to have worked with three research groups during my time so far in college. Most recently, I have been working with the Britton Lab within the Joint Quantum Institute to help them hopefully design a more easily scalable quantum computer using photonic qubits.

As a freshman, I volunteered for the ISS-CREAM research group to help troubleshoot their last balloon experiment and repair the equipment for future use.

In the fall of 2019, I was employed by the University of Maryland’s Radiation Facilities to work on our nuclear reactor and linear accelerator. With them I completed a number of projects in python, MATLAB, and AutoCAD including simulations, interactive GUI's, and the design of lab equipment. I also completed a Trustworthiness and Reliability (T&R) application,who from, and received training to operate our reactor, LINAC, and a variety of radiation detection equipment.

In 2020, I first introduced myself to data science through Google’s Machine Learning Crash Course, Stephen Boyd’s text on Convex Optimization, and a number of smaller projects I completed along the way in TensorFlow and Scikit-Learn. I then used the Kepler Exoplanet Search to develop a deep learning algorithm capable of identifying exoplanets based on the observations of nearly 10,000 exoplanet candidates collected by NASA and their Kepler telescope.

This winter, I used a Raspberry Pi to broaden the scope my programming abilities. So far I have completed projects teaching me about API’s, web scraping, threading, and GPIO programming (among others). It is exciting to see rapid improvements to my programming fluency and ability to quickly pick up new packages to implement in my projects.

Recent Projects

2D Ising Model

    For this report and programming assignment, I recreated the 2D Ising Model from thermodynamics and used the Metropolis–Hastings algorithm to solve for the system's average energy and magnetization after thermalization.

Download This Project!
Wavelet Analysis

     In the fall of 2020, I briefly assisted the Britton Lab in the Joint Quantum Institute to help them learn about and predict the thermal drift in their prototype quantum computer. I did so by performing wavelet analysis with a complex morlet wavelet. In particular, I made use of the PyWavelets and Scaleogram packages for python.

Reactor Status Board

    Using python, I created an interactive status board to be used in the University of Maryland's nuclear reactor. Through the GUI, it is possible to communicate with the facility’s experimental ports directly from the control room. Additionally, the status board is capable of tracking and recording fuel movement between the core and the neighboring storage containers.

Quantum Computing (Qiskit)

Beyond learning the quantum logic gates I have been successful in using qiskit to write my own algorithms. Quantum Optics

    In this paper I discuss the application of quantum optics to encode information into a qubit through gaussian pulses. I then demonstrated how we can use Fourier analysis to predict the net result from applying a composite wavefunction of multiple pulses.
